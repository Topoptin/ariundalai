Lake Khuvsgul, known as the "Dark Blue Pearl of Mongolia," is one of the country's most breathtaking destinations. Nestled in northern Mongolia, this pristine alpine lake is surrounded by towering mountains, dense taiga forests, and offers crystal-clear waters that rival any natural wonder in the world. Choosing the right accommodation is essential for making the most of your visit to this spectacular region.

In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore the top resorts and camps around Lake Khuvsgul, helping you find the perfect base for your adventure. Whether you're seeking luxury amenities, traditional Mongolian experiences, or family-friendly comfort, there's an option for every traveler.

## Why Lake Khuvsgul?

Before diving into the accommodation options, let's understand what makes Lake Khuvsgul so special:

- **Pristine Beauty** — One of the world's oldest and deepest freshwater lakes
- **Rich Biodiversity** — Home to unique wildlife and rare bird species
- **Cultural Heritage** — Traditional Mongolian nomadic culture and Tsaatan reindeer herders
- **Adventure Activities** — Horseback riding, hiking, kayaking, and winter ice festivals
- **Peaceful Retreat** — Escape from city life into nature's tranquility

## Top Resorts & Camps Overview

Lake Khuvsgul's western shore is dotted with various camps and resorts, each offering unique experiences and amenities. Prices typically range from 100,000 to 400,000 Mongolian tögrög per night, with varying levels of luxury and services.

## Planning Your Visit

### Best Time to Visit

**Summer (June-September):** Peak season with warm weather, full camp operations, and the most activities. July-August is ideal for swimming.

**Winter (Ice Festival):** Unique experience with frozen lake activities, though fewer camps operate. Special cultural events and stunning ice formations.

### Getting There

1. **Fly to Murun** from Ulaanbaatar (1-hour flight)
2. **Drive to Lake Khuvsgul** (2-3 hours from Murun)
3. Most camps can arrange transportation or provide directions

### Booking Tips

- Book 2-3 months in advance for peak season (July-August)
- Contact camps directly for best rates and availability
- Ask about package deals including meals and activities
- Confirm what's included in the room rate

## What to Pack

Regardless of which camp you choose, bring:

- Warm layers (temperatures drop at night)
- Rain gear (sudden weather changes)
- Sun protection (high altitude, intense UV)
- Comfortable hiking shoes
- Camera equipment
- Insect repellent
- Personal toiletries
- Cash (limited ATM access)

## Sustainable Tourism

Lake Khuvsgul is a precious natural resource. When visiting:

- Choose camps with environmental commitments (like Art 88 Resort)
- Minimize waste and dispose properly
- Respect wildlife and local communities
- Support local businesses
- Stay on marked trails
- Conserve water and energy
